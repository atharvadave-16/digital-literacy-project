📄 Case Study 

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources like banks or well-known companies.

A typical phishing attack begins when a user receives a message claiming that there is an urgent issue with their bank account or that they have won a reward. The message contains a link that directs the user to a fake website designed to look like the original. When the user enters their login credentials or financial information, the attacker captures this data.

Students and young individuals are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking on links and sharing personal information online.
